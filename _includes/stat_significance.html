Statistical Significance

Sampling 50 random contacts from a database does not necessarily mean that that sample reflects the breakdown of the entire database. We can use a bit of statistics to reason about how accurate it would be to assume the same breakdown for the the 670 contacts in UNHCR's database.

For our sample, we calculated that with a desired confidence level of 95%, our margin of error would be 13%. In other words, we are 95% sure that the actual database will reflect our sample within a margin of error of plus or minus 13%. So with 95% assurance, there is anywhere from 234 to 302 contacts in the database that need updating.
